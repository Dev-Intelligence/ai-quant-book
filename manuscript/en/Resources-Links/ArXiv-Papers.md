# Academic Papers for Quantitative Trading

> This document collects academic papers related to quantitative trading, machine learning, and multi-agent systems for in-depth study.

---

## Must-Read Classics (Peer-Reviewed / Textbooks)

The following are peer-reviewed or widely recognized foundational works in quantitative trading research:

### Market Microstructure

| Publication | Author/Source | Core Content |
|-------------|---------------|--------------|
| **Market Microstructure Theory** | O'Hara (1995), Blackwell | Foundational text on market microstructure theory |
| **Optimal Execution of Portfolio Transactions** | Almgren & Chriss (2000), JRM | Classic model for optimal execution |
| **A Closed-Form Solution for Optimal Execution** | Almgren (2003), JRM | Analytical solution to optimal execution problem |

### Factor Investing and Asset Pricing

| Publication | Author/Source | Core Content |
|-------------|---------------|--------------|
| **Common Risk Factors in Returns on Stocks and Bonds** | Fama & French (1993), JFE | Fama-French three-factor model |
| **A Five-Factor Asset Pricing Model** | Fama & French (2015), JFE | Five-factor model (adds profitability and investment factors) |
| **Momentum** | Jegadeesh & Titman (1993), JF | Empirical study of momentum effect |

### Backtesting Pitfalls and Strategy Evaluation

| Publication | Author/Source | Core Content |
|-------------|---------------|--------------|
| **Pseudo-Mathematics and Financial Charlatanism** | Marcos López de Prado (2014) | Warning about backtest overfitting and false discoveries |
| **The Deflated Sharpe Ratio** | Bailey & López de Prado (2014) | Sharpe ratio corrected for multiple testing bias |
| **Advances in Financial Machine Learning** | López de Prado (2018), Wiley | Practical guide to financial ML (book) |
